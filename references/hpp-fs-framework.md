# HPP Feasibility Study Framework

## 1. Project Overview and Technical Feasibility

Extract:

- Location, river basin, nearest load center, grid zone.
- HPP type: run-of-river, reservoir, pumped storage, cascade, hybrid.
- Installed capacity, firm capacity, unit number/type, design head, design flow, annual generation.
- Dam type: RCC, CFRD, embankment, arch, gravity, diversion weir.
- Storage: gross, active, dead, regulation period, flood-control role.
- Civil works: dam height, spillway, intake, desander, headrace, penstock, powerhouse, tailrace, diversion works, access roads, transmission line.

Core checks:

- Hydrology: data length, station quality, catchment scaling, missing data, climate trend, glacial contribution, flow duration curve, monthly seasonality, P50/P75/P90 energy.
- Floods: construction flood, design flood, check flood, PMF where relevant.
- Sediment: bed load/suspended load, reservoir sedimentation, flushing, abrasion risk.
- Geology: rock mass, faults, permeability, grouting, seismicity, slope stability, tunnel class, liquefaction.
- Equipment: turbine type, cavitation, part-load operation, ramping, AGC, black-start if relevant.

## 2. Environmental, Social, and Permitting Feasibility

Assess:

- EIA/ESIA status and gap to IFC Performance Standards and ADB Safeguard Policy where international financing is expected.
- E-flow, fish migration, aquatic habitat, wetlands, protected areas, biodiversity critical habitat.
- Land acquisition, resettlement, livelihoods, fishermen, irrigation users, cultural heritage.
- Stakeholder engagement, grievance mechanism, emergency preparedness, dam safety disclosure.
- Permits: water use, EIA approval, land allocation, forestry conversion, construction, grid connection, generation license where applicable.

## 3. Economic and Financial Feasibility

Require:

- CAPEX by package: civil works, hydro-mechanical, electro-mechanical, transmission, roads, owner costs, IDC, contingency, taxes/duties.
- OPEX: fixed O&M, major maintenance, insurance, water fees, land fees, concession fees, staffing.
- Revenue stack: capacity, energy, ancillary service, balancing, direct industrial offtake, carbon/green certificates if available.
- Model outputs: NPV, project IRR, equity IRR, DSCR average/minimum, payback, LCOE.
- Sensitivities: hydrology, CAPEX, delay, FX, interest, tariff/capacity price, OPEX, availability, E-flow.

## 4. Legal, Policy, Institutional, and Market Feasibility

Review:

- Market route: capacity agreement, PPA, auction award, direct agreement, BOT/BOOT/concession, corporate offtake.
- Land rights, water rights, grid rights, state support, tax and customs incentives.
- Change in law, force majeure, termination, indexation, dispute forum, step-in rights, assignment, lender direct agreement.
- Interface with KEGOC, FSC/RFC, KOREM, MOE, water authorities, Akimat, environmental regulator.

## 5. Risk Matrix

Score each risk by probability, impact, owner, mitigation, residual risk, and decision action. Cover technical, hydrology, geology, climate, E&S, permitting, land, grid, market, legal, FX, interest rate, construction, O&M, cross-border water, and political risks.

## 6. Dual-Track Strategy

Investor/developer:

- Bid/no-bid view, valuation, due diligence list, financing plan, partner map, bankability amendments, auction price strategy.

EPC contractor:

- Bid strategy, exclusions, geotechnical baseline, construction method, schedule, local subcontracting, equipment sourcing, interface matrix, claims and variation triggers.
