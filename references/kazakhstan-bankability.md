# Kazakhstan Hydropower Bankability Reference

## Market Structure

For Kazakhstan HPP work, distinguish renewable energy PPA structures from maneuverable generation capacity-market structures.

Key institutions:

- Ministry of Energy (MOE): energy policy, tariff caps, auction framework, market rules.
- FSC/RFC single buyer: centralized settlement and buyer role for relevant market instruments.
- KOREM: market operator and auction platform.
- KEGOC: transmission system operator, dispatch, grid connection, capacity certification, system services.
- Ministry of Water Resources and Irrigation and water committees: water use, basin planning, irrigation coordination, IWRM.
- Environmental regulator and local Akimat: EIA, land, forestry/category conversion, local approvals.

## Capacity Market Checklist

Review these points in every Kazakhstan capacity-market HPP:

| Issue | Bankability Test |
|---|---|
| Capacity agreement term | Is the term long enough to recover capital, normally 15 years minimum and preferably 20+ years for high-dam HPP? |
| Capacity tariff | Is it indexed and sufficient in KZT/MW-month to cover debt service and equity return? |
| Energy revenue | Is energy paid separately, and are curtailment/grid downtime rules clear? |
| AGC/balancing/ancillary revenue | Are technical requirements and settlement rules defined? |
| Certification | Are KEGOC tests, regulation range, availability tests, derating, and retesting rules objective? |
| Delay and EOT | Are extensions available for force majeure, government delay, grid delay, archaeological/environmental discoveries, and unforeseeable geological conditions? |
| Change in law | Is compensation available for Kazakhstan law changes affecting cost, tax, revenue, or permits? |
| Indexation | Is there construction and operation period indexation to CPI, FX, and foreign-currency debt exposure? |
| Termination compensation | Does it cover debt, break costs, equity, and reasonable return for public-sector default? |
| Dispute resolution | Is AIFC or international arbitration available instead of only domestic courts? |
| Offtaker support | Are payment security, reserve fund, sovereign support, or other credit enhancement mechanisms available? |

## Water and E&S Bankability

Treat water allocation as a core finance risk, not only a permit issue. Confirm:

- Water use permit and basin authority support.
- Seasonal conflict management with irrigation, especially summer low-flow periods.
- Environmental flow model under dry, average, and wet hydrology.
- Ramsar/protected-area screening where downstream wetlands or lakes are affected.
- Fish passage, sediment continuity, reservoir habitat conversion, and biodiversity offset/management plan.
- Emergency action plan (EAP), dam-break analysis, flood warning, and community engagement plan.

## Bankability Rating

Use:

- Bankable: key data complete, permits achievable, offtake financeable, risk allocation lender acceptable.
- Conditionally bankable: technically viable but needs legal, E&S, hydrology, or tariff amendments before debt financing.
- Not bankable under current terms: major unresolved revenue, legal, E&S, or construction risks prevent financing.
